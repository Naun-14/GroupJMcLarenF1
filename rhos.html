Racing History: Other Series

Can-Am

McLaren's first racing car, the M1, was built "from the rubber up" by Bruce McLaren. It found success in North America and Europe, leading to dominance in the Can-Am Series starting from 1966.

USAC

McLaren entered the Indianapolis 500 in 1970 with the M15 car, marking the beginning of the team's involvement in American open-wheel racing history.

IndyCar Series

In 2017, McLaren returned to IndyCar with Fernando Alonso at the Indy 500. Despite an engine failure near the end, Alonso's performance was widely celebrated.

Extreme E

Joining Extreme E in 2022, McLaren fielded a team featuring Tanner Foust and Emma Gilmour, achieving their first podium at the Energy X-Prix and showcasing gender diversity in motorsport.

Formula E

In 2022, McLaren acquired the Mercedes-EQ Formula E team, launching a new era with strong drivers and a fast start, highlighted by a pole position and fastest lap early in the season.

Endurance Racing

McLaren is reviewing Hypercar class regulations for a 2027 FIA World Endurance Championship (WEC) and IMSA WeatherTech GTP entry. As of April 2025, McLaren confirmed its participation under LMDh regulations starting in 2027.
