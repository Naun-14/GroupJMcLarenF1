Racing History: Other Series

Can-Am

McLaren's first racing car designed and built "from the rubber up" by Bruce McLaren Motor Racing was the M1. The car with a small block Oldsmobile had immediate success driven by Bruce McLaren. The car was raced in North America and Europe in 1964 in various A sports and United States Road Racing Championship events. In 1965 the team car was the M1A prototype from which the production Elva M1As were based. In late 1965, the M1B (also known as Mk2) was the team car for the North American races at the end of the year. For the Can-Am Series, which started in 1966, McLaren created the M3 which Bruce and Chris Amon drove – customer cars also appeared in several races in the 1966 season.

USAC

McLaren first contested the USAC's Indianapolis 500 race in 1970, encouraged by tyre supplier Goodyear. With the M15 car, Bruce, Chris Amon, and Denny Hulme entered, but after Amon withdrew and Hulme was burned in practice, Peter Revson and Carl Williams replaced them. Revson retired and Williams finished seventh.

IndyCar Series

In 2017, McLaren entered the Indianapolis 500 with F1 driver Fernando Alonso in a Honda-powered McLaren-branded Andretti IndyCar. Alonso qualified 5th, led 27 laps, but retired 21 laps from the end due to engine failure. He received a standing ovation for his debut performance.

Extreme E

McLaren entered Extreme E in 2022 with Tanner Foust and Emma Gilmour, the first female factory driver for McLaren. They achieved their first podium at the Energy X-Prix, finishing second. Gilmour became the first woman to reach a podium with McLaren. The team ended the season 5th overall.

Formula E

In 2022, McLaren acquired the Mercedes-EQ Formula E team and debuted as NEOM McLaren Formula E Team with Nissan powertrains. René Rast and Jake Hughes joined as drivers. The team debuted in Mexico, with Hughes finishing fifth. At Diriyah, Hughes secured McLaren’s first pole and Rast scored the team’s first fastest lap.
